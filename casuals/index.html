How do I get paid?

Casual pay dates and timecard approvals

Casual pay dates fall fortnightly on a Thursday and your hours must be submitted and approved by your supervisor no later than Monday 4.00pm of the casual pay week. Ensure you submit your timecard no later than Friday 9.00am (before the Monday cut off) to give your supervisor sufficient time to approve. 

How to enter and use timecards

You are required to submit a fortnightly timecard through Themis to claim payment for your work. Please follow these step by step instructions:

  • Log into Themis using your unimelb User ID and password.
  • Click on ‘UOM Staff Self Service’.
  • Click on ‘Timecard’.
  • Click on ‘Create Timecard’.
  • Select the Pay Period you wish to allocate hours to. Hours may be entered retrospectively in the five pay periods prior to the current pay period. Please enter your hours on the days you worked them. 
  • Select the Template for the contract you are entering hours for. For teaching contracts this will include the subject code or name, and should also include the year (eg. 2016) and semester (eg. SM1). For research/professional contracts this will include an activity description (eg. Research Support/Admin & Professional Support).
  • Click on ‘Apply Template’ to populate the contract details.
  • If the ‘Job Performed’ section is blank, please choose Academic Teaching and Research from the drop-down menu.
  • Scroll to the right and enter hours worked. Themis will not allow you to enter hours outside your contract duration and will generate the following error message: Error: The following time entry rule has been violated (Check for valid contract). Please contact your Supervisor to discuss a contract extension if required.
  • If you are entering hours for more than one subject – you can now select the template that relates to the next subject for which you are entering hours and click on ‘Apply Template’. This will populate the details of this contract on the lines below, adding more lines if necessary. Scroll to the right and enter hours worked.
  • When all hours have been entered, click on ‘Continue’ to go to a summary of the timecard you are submitting. Double check the details.
  • Last important step - Click on ‘Submit’ to send to your supervisor for approval. 
  • If you are entering hours in more than one pay period – start over and follow the above steps to submit a new timecard for hours worked in a different pay period.

Facilities

Staff Cards and Building Access

Your University staff card is your access to the Melbourne School of Design, including all external doors, staff spaces, classrooms and computer labs. Your staff card is also your on-campus identification. Carry it with you at all times. Do not let anyone else use your staff card. 

If your contract is renewed you will need to log a request via the Facilities online request form (see below) to have your access reinstated.

• To have your card printed, take a copy of your contract and photo ID to Access Control located at 213 Grattan Street.
• To have your card activated, log a request via the Facilities page on the ABP intranet (You will need your username and password to login).
• Please allow 72 hours for the request to process and your card to begin working.

Lectern Keys

Lectern (LEC) keys open lecterns and computer cabinets across campus. Under the new operating model, LEC keys are now the responsibility of the subject coordinator or senior tutor to distribute and collect from their tutors at the beginning and end of semesters. The facilities office no longer has the resources to hand out the LEC keys to each individual tutor. You return your LEC keys to the facilities office at the end of your contract or when you leave the University.

Lockers

Lockers will be allocated to eligible staff each semester based on order of application. Preference is provided to casual and sessional staff who do not have access to the bike store or do not have a permanent work point with secure storage.

To request a locker, please contact the Facilities team and include the following information:

  • full name
  • staff ID number
  • position (ie: tutor)
  • subject (ie: ENVS10003 Constructing Environments)
  • contract details (start/end date)
  • name of your supervisor
  • contact details (mobile phone and email address)
  • locker location preference (MSD Bldg Level 4, Baldwin Spencer Bldg G42)

All staff must supply their own padlock which must feature a shackle with a minimum diameter of 6mm. Padlocks with a smaller shackle will not work on these lockers.

Mail Room / Stationery / Printing Facilities

Mail Room

The mail room is on Level 2, Room 202 of the MSD Bldg. Only senior tutors and subject coordinators will be allocated pigeon holes in the mail room. If you are a senior tutor, please ensure you check your pigeon hole regularly. 

Stationery

Stocked stationery items are on an open bookshelf nearest the corridor in the mail room. Please use items sparingly and for legitimate business purposes only. Items are ordered and restocked on a weekly basis only. 

Printing Facilities

In the MSD Bldg, there are two staff print rooms on floors 3 and 4 that are swipe card access only. The Student Print Room is located on Level 1, Room 127. There is a printer in Baldwin Spencer Bldg near the staff kitchen. 

Desk space

Hot desks are located in G42 Baldwin Spencer Bldg and outside lifts on levels 3 & 4 of the MSD Bldg. 

Teaching Spaces

Please remember to return furniture to original locations and do not remove chairs/tables from rooms. Supplies of stationery such as whiteboard markers/erasers etc. are for the benefit of all teaching in that space, so please do not remove these items at the end of your session.

Training

OHS Training

All staff involved in supervising staff or teaching students (tutors, senior tutors, lecturers) will need to complete the three online OHS training modules below. This must be completed every two years.

  1. OHS & Environmental Roles and responsibilities for Supervisors and Managers
  2. Risk Management
  3. Incident Investigation.

Tutor Training – Teaching and Learning Unit

ABP offers Teaching and Learning training to our sessional staff twice a year, at the start of each semester. All sessional teaching staff are required to attend this training every five years. The exact dates of training sessions are advertised to subject coordinators in the first instance who will advise you of these dates.

Teaching and Learning training sessions run for two hours and staff are paid to attend.
